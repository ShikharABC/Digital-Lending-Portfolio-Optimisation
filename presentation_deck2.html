Business Context
The Digital Lending Dilemma
Scale and speed enabled rapid growth — but evolving borrower behavior and rising acquisition costs demand a smarter risk approach.
⚡

Speed vs. Portfolio Quality Trade-off

Digital channels deliver 1–2 day approvals but carry ~19% default rates — 2× that of branch originations. Faster growth may be loading hidden systemic risk into the book.

🔍

Siloed Data, Blind Risk Spots

Repayment, behavioral, and origination signals live in separate systems. Risk drivers are identified reactively at DPD-30 — not 60 days earlier when intervention is still effective.

💲

Flat Pricing Subsidizes Risk

Uniform rate cards under-price weak credits and over-price strong ones. DTI stress and income instability remain unpriced, compressing net interest margins on the riskiest cohorts.

📊

Leadership Visibility Gap

No forward-looking portfolio health view exists for senior leadership. Delinquency is surfaced only after it happens — not 45–90 days earlier via behavioral and financial signals.

🎯 Central Problem Statement

How can FinVista leverage end-to-end customer and transaction data to strengthen credit risk assessment, enable early detection of delinquencies, optimize pricing and product strategies, and drive sustainable, risk-adjusted portfolio growth?

② How do acquisition channels affect portfolio quality & unit economics?
③ Which products deliver the strongest risk-adjusted returns?
④ How should pricing & tenure be tailored by segment?

Early Warning System
Three-Tier EWS — Act Before DPD-30
Proactive intervention framework detecting ~74% of eventual defaults 45+ days ahead of formal delinquency.
Tier 1 — Green Watch
60–90 days lead
Triggers: Single missed payment · DTI drift >10% · Spending shock >2× average
→ Soft outreach (SMS/app nudge) · Flag for next-cycle reassessment
Tier 2 — Amber Alert
30–60 days lead
Triggers: 2nd consecutive partial payment · CF volatility upgrade → High · LTI rising >30%
→ Inbound call + restructuring offer · EMI holiday (≤2 months)
Tier 3 — Red Alert
0–30 days lead
Triggers: DPD 30+ · Partial payment <50% EMI for 2+ months · Balance deterioration
→ Escalate to Recoveries within 48h · NPA provisioning trigger

Policy Recommendations
Six Targeted Actions
Data-backed, operationally executable policies designed to reduce credit losses while preserving origination quality.
🚦
A — Score-Based Hard Cutoffs
Mandatory gates: ≥580 for Personal Loan ≤₹5L · ≥620 for larger tickets · ≥560 for BNPL · ≥600 + cashflow for SME. Eliminates the Very High Risk band from originations entirely.
−₹4–6 Cr credit losses
📏
B — DTI Overlay Decline Rule
Auto-decline: DTI proxy >2.0 AND credit_quality = Poor, regardless of score. Directly targets the most predictive engineered interaction — interest_x_dti.
−₹3–4 Cr credit losses
💲
C — Risk-Based Pricing Grid
3-band structure: Prime (720+) at 11–13% · Near-Prime (600–720) at 15–18% · Sub-Prime (500–600) at 20–24%. Prices in expected loss; rewards good borrowers.
+₹5–7 Cr interest income
⏰
D — Tenure Caps by Segment
Sub-prime borrowers (score 500–600): maximum tenure capped at 18 months (vs current 36 months). Reduces exposure duration and improves loss-given-default in stressed scenarios.
↓ Loss severity on defaults
📱
E — Digital Channel Overlays
+50 score requirement for loans >₹3L via app channel · mandatory income verification (AA consent) for >₹1L · reduce pre-approved limits for first-loan Tier-3 profiles.
−₹2–3 Cr digital NPA
📊
F — Leadership Risk Dashboard
Monthly monitoring: 30-DPD Rate (>8% → review) · EWS Amber Coverage (>12% → scale) · Score Migration Index · Vintage Default Rate · CAC-to-CLV Ratio (>0.35 → reprice).
